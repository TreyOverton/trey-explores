# Geometry

Geometry is where this exploration started — a question about Euclid's axioms that opened into non-Euclidean geometries, then Riemannian geometry, and eventually into the mathematical foundation for General Relativity.

## The Core Arc

The 2,000-year story of geometry is really a story about one axiom: Euclid's fifth postulate, the parallel postulate. For centuries mathematicians tried to prove it from the other four. They couldn't — because it's genuinely independent. Replacing it produces consistent but radically different geometries.

**[Euclidean Axioms](euclidean-axioms.md)** — Euclid's five postulates, why the fifth is different, and the 2,000-year controversy over whether it's truly independent.

**[Non-Euclidean Geometries](non-euclidean-geometries.md)** — What happens when you replace the parallel postulate. Hyperbolic geometry (infinitely many parallels, negative curvature), elliptic/spherical geometry (no parallels, positive curvature), and the realization that these are just as valid as Euclid's flat plane.

## What's Ahead

The exploration path continues through Riemannian geometry — the general framework for spaces of arbitrary curvature that Riemann developed in his 1854 habilitation lecture. This is the mathematical language General Relativity is written in. See the [exploration queue](../../exploration-queue.md) for the full roadmap.

## Connections

The leap from geometry to physics: [non-Euclidean geometries](non-euclidean-geometries.md) showed that curvature is a real, consistent mathematical concept. [Minkowski spacetime](../special-relativity/minkowski-spacetime.md) modified Euclidean distance measurement for spacetime. Curving that Minkowski geometry gives you General Relativity.
