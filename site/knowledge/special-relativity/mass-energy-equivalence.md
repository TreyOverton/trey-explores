# Mass-Energy Equivalence (E = mc²)

**Status:** Understood

Mass and energy are the same thing, measured in different units. E = mc² is not a conversion *process* — it's a statement that mass *is* energy, expressed in units that require a conversion factor.

## Why *c*² Is a Unit Conversion Artifact

Energy is measured in joules: kg·m²/s². Mass is measured in kilograms. To get from kg to kg·m²/s², you need to multiply by something with units of m²/s² — a velocity squared. *c* is which velocity because it is the fundamental [spacetime](spacetime.md) conversion constant, the ratio between space and time.

In natural units where *c* = 1 (and distances are measured in light-seconds), the equation reduces to E = m. The bookkeeping disappears entirely. The "dramatic" equation is an artifact of SI units — historically useful units that happen to measure space and time in different scales.

## Why SI Units Make It Look Clean

This is a subtlety worth noting: SI units were deliberately constructed to be *coherent* — base units combine without extra conversion factors in most equations. This is why E = mc² is clean rather than E = (some ugly constant) × mc². The coherence is a designed property of the unit system, not a deep fact about the equation.
