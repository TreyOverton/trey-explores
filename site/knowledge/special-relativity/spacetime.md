# Spacetime

**Status:** Understood

Spacetime is the unification of three spatial dimensions and one time dimension into a single four-dimensional structure. Events don't happen at a place *and* a time — they happen at a point in spacetime.

## Why Unification Was Necessary

Before [Special Relativity](sr-overview.md), space and time were treated as independent. Newton's physics assumed absolute time ticking at the same rate for everyone, with space as a separate stage. But SR showed that observers in different [inertial frames](inertial-frames.md) disagree about both spatial distances and time intervals — what one observer sees as purely a time difference, another sees as a mix of space and time. The quantities aren't independently invariant. Only when combined into four-dimensional spacetime do you get invariant quantities (the spacetime interval).

## The Core Idea

Space and time are different directions in the same four-dimensional structure, related by the [speed of light](speed-of-light.md) as a conversion factor. Moving through space affects your passage through time. This isn't metaphor — it's the geometry of [Minkowski spacetime](minkowski-spacetime.md).

## Related Concepts
