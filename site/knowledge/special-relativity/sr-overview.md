# Special Relativity — Overview

Special Relativity is Einstein's 1905 theory describing how space and time behave for observers moving at constant velocity relative to each other. It resolved a fundamental conflict between Newtonian mechanics (which assumed absolute time) and Maxwell's electrodynamics (which predicted a fixed speed of light).

## What It States

Physics works the same in every [inertial frame](inertial-frames.md), and [the speed of light](speed-of-light.md) is the same for all observers regardless of their motion. From these two postulates, everything else follows: [simultaneity is relative](simultaneity.md), [time dilates](time-dilation.md), lengths contract, and [mass and energy are equivalent](mass-energy-equivalence.md).

## What Problem It Solved

Before SR, there was a contradiction: Maxwell's equations predicted electromagnetic waves travel at a specific speed (*c*), but Newtonian mechanics said velocities should add. If you're on a train moving at half the speed of light and shine a flashlight forward, Newton says the light moves at 1.5*c* relative to the ground. Maxwell says it moves at *c*. They can't both be right. SR resolved this by keeping Maxwell and modifying Newton — time and space themselves adjust to keep *c* constant.

## Key Relationships

- Built on two postulates: [The Two Postulates](two-postulates.md)
- Operates within [Minkowski Spacetime](minkowski-spacetime.md) — the flat spacetime geometry of SR
- [Why SR is "Special"](why-sr-is-special.md) — its scope boundary is curvature, not speed
- SR is contained within General Relativity as a local approximation valid where spacetime curvature is negligible
