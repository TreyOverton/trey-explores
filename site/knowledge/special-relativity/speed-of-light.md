# Speed of Light as a Universal Speed Limit

The speed of light (*c* ≈ 299,792,458 m/s) is not just the speed at which light happens to travel — it is a structural constant of spacetime. It is the speed at which all massless particles travel and the upper limit for the speed of any information, causation, or massive object.

## Why *c* and Not Some Other Speed

*c* isn't special because of light. Light is special because of *c*. The constant emerges from the structure of [spacetime](spacetime.md) itself — it's the conversion factor between space and time. In the [four-velocity](time-dilation.md) framework, every object moves through spacetime at exactly *c* at all times. What changes is the direction of that motion: more through space means less through time, and vice versa.

## Connection to Mass-Energy Equivalence

In [E = mc²](mass-energy-equivalence.md), *c*² functions as a unit conversion factor between mass and energy. It looks dramatic in SI units, but in natural units where *c* = 1, it's simply E = m. The constant is the same *c* — the fundamental spacetime ratio.
