# Inertial Frames

An inertial frame is a reference frame in which an object not subject to any net force moves in a straight line at constant speed. Newton's first law holds. No acceleration, no rotation — just uniform motion or rest.

## Why It Matters

Inertial frames are the stage on which [Special Relativity](sr-overview.md) operates. SR's [two postulates](two-postulates.md) specifically claim that physics is the same in *every* inertial frame, and that the [speed of light](speed-of-light.md) is constant across all of them. The restriction to inertial frames is what makes SR "special" — General Relativity extends to accelerating and gravitational frames.

## The Key Insight

There is no experiment you can perform inside a sealed lab moving at constant velocity that will tell you your absolute speed. You can only measure speed *relative to* something else. This isn't a practical limitation — it's a fundamental feature of how the universe works.
