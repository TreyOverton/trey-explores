# The Two Postulates of Special Relativity

[Special Relativity](sr-overview.md) is built on exactly two assumptions. Everything else — [time dilation](time-dilation.md), [length contraction](length-contraction.md), [mass-energy equivalence](mass-energy-equivalence.md), [relativity of simultaneity](simultaneity.md) — is a logical consequence of these two postulates.

## Postulate 1: The Principle of Relativity

The laws of physics are the same in every [inertial frame](inertial-frames.md). No experiment performed inside a sealed, uniformly moving lab can determine your absolute velocity. There is no preferred frame.

This isn't new with Einstein — Galileo articulated a version of it. What was new was taking it *seriously* in combination with the second postulate.

## Postulate 2: The Constancy of the Speed of Light

The [speed of light](speed-of-light.md) in vacuum is the same for all inertial observers, regardless of the motion of the light source or the observer.

This is the radical one. It contradicts everyday intuition about how velocities add. If you're moving toward a light source, you'd expect to measure the light arriving faster. You don't. It arrives at exactly *c*.

## Why Two Postulates Create a Revolution

Accepting both postulates simultaneously forces you to give up absolute time. If light speed is invariant and physics is the same in all frames, then [simultaneity must be relative](simultaneity.md) — two events that are simultaneous in one frame aren't simultaneous in another. Time dilation and length contraction follow mathematically.
