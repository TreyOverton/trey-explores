## Riemannian Geometry

The mathematical machinery needed for General Relativity:

- Manifolds (spaces that are locally flat but globally curved)
- Intrinsic vs. extrinsic curvature
- The metric tensor (g_μν) — measuring distances on a curved surface
- How the metric becomes a *field* (varies from point to point)
- Geodesics (generalization of "straight line" on a curved surface)
- Parallel transport (moving a vector along a curve without rotating it)
- Covariant derivatives (differentiating vectors on a curved manifold)
- Christoffel symbols / connection coefficients
- The Riemann curvature tensor (R^ρ_σμν)
- Ricci tensor (contracted curvature)
- Ricci scalar (further contraction)
- Einstein tensor (G_μν)
